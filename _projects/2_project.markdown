A Supernova is the explosive death of massive stars. A simplistic view of the processes leading to such a burst is due to an imbalance between gravity and internal pressure due to nuclear fusion. As the star runs out of nuclear fuel, gravity takes over. Supernovae are classified based on the attributes seen in their optical spectra.
